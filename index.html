Keep the ITU feeling - Focus on well-being

Christian Skovsgaard Rieck for Board of Directors at ITU

The ITU feeling is currently possible because our university is small. However, ITU expands regularly, creating division and putting the ITU feeling to the test. I will fight to ensure great cohesion and collaboration between students and staff and between study lines and departments.

A better ITU

The students need a chance to understand the lecturers, and the lecturers need a way to understand the students. We need ways to communicate face to face with each other. It could be at events where both staff/students feel welcomed, such as a calm Thursday bar. To encourage informal and comfortable talks to help improve the learning environment. Happy students and staff might also improve the already great research of ITU â€“ which could rank ITU higher.

Well-being of students

ITU is filled with students, and there is an increased interest in spending time at the campus. This results in overcrowded areas - when trying to find a table to study or in line at ScrollBar to get a drink after a week's work. This complicates many things for students. The well-being of students is one of my main concerns. There are many factors to consider, the study- and social environment, and the mental state of students. There is a lot of pressure on ITU students, which can be alleviated by using different methods, such as removing a portion of the considerable amounts of mandatory assignments, etc. The students need the possibility to extend their study period, which is not an easy process at ITU yet. We need to uphold the quality of education without compromising our well-being. I will try to accommodate this problem in a way that takes the students' needs into account - by bringing new ideas from the students' perspective to the ITU strategy.

Future expansions are not necessarily equal to a worse ITU

If the proposal to create a new ITU campus in Vejle passes, I will also fight to ensure that we keep the ITU feel whether you are located in Vejle or Copenhagen. This can be done with cross-campus activities to ensure we all feel welcome at both locations.

I would love to explain my issues or hear your ideas, so please reach out if you want a talk over a nice cup of coffee in Analog. You can find my contact information at the bottom of this page.

  • We need a great atmosphere in order to thrive as students, this includes the social, learning, and teaching aspect.

  • If you elect me - I will fight to keep ITU great. I will love to hear your inputs for the long term goals.

  • The ITU feeling is worth fighting for!

Who am I

My name is Christian, and I'm a student at ITU. I love to be busy, and therefore I also actively seek new challenges. I have been a tutor for the new BSc students three years in a row. Moreover, I am also part of ScrollBar, where I currently sit on the board and control the finances. However, I am stepping down to focus on joining the board of directors. It is great to see how we as students strive to thrive and how engaged we are to ensure we all feel welcome both through organizations and especially with the volunteering tutors/buddies. I love adventure, and I try to seek new experiences as often as possible. This is also why I scuba dive and have taken several courses through the years. If you know of a great adventure, hit me up!

What do I study

I am currently interested in picking the software engineering specialization since I enjoy being involved in decision-making and management.

Where do I work

Other causes

I would love to help keep ITU great by getting close to the long-term decision-making. Therefore, joining the board of directors is a perfect match to fulfill these goals. The Student Council is already trying to bring focus to mental health and well-being. I would love to bring that further up to the decision-making to ensure such an important issue gets the attention it needs. Moreover, I want to promote climate awareness at ITU to create a more sustainable environment at the university. I want to be proactive in tackling problems before they arise or while they are still latent. I believe this is key to improving ITU - however, we can not forget the already existing problems.

How to vote

  1. Pick your candidate(s).
  2. Go to ITU either at the 29th or 30th of November between 10:00-14:00.
  3. Find the voting tables in atrium. Remember ID card (student card is allowed).
  4. Fill out the voting papers which were handed to you.
  5. You are done! Thanks for voting.
