Keep the ITU feeling - My key issue

Christian Skovsgaard Rieck for Board of Directors at ITU

The ITU feeling is currently possible because our university is rather small. However, ITU expands regularly and it will create division and put the ITU feeling to test. I will fight to ensure great cohesion and collaboration between both students and staff, but also between study lines and departments.

The students need a chance to understand the lecturers, and the lecturers need a chance to understand the students. We need ways to communicate face to face with each other, it could be at events where both staff/students feel welcomed, such as a calm Thursday bar. This encourages relaxed and comfortable talks to help improve the learning environment. Happy students and staff might also improve the already great research of ITU â€“ which could rank ITU higher.

ITU is filled with students and there is an increased interest in the study life. This results in overcrowded areas - when trying to find a table to study or in line at ScrollBar to get a drink after a week's work. The well-being of students are one of my main concerns. There are many factors to take into account, the study- and social environment, but also the mental state of students. I will try to accomodate this problem in a way that takes the students' needs into account.

If the proposal to create a new ITU campus in Vejle passes - then I will also fight to ensure that we keep the ITU feel no matter if you are located in Vejle or Copenhagen. This can be done with cross-campus activities to ensure we all feel welcome at both locations.

I would love to explain or hear your ideas, so please reach out if you want a talk over a nice cup of coffee in Analog. You can find my contact information at the bottom of this page.

  • We need a great atmosphere in order to thrive as students, this includes the social, learning, and teaching aspect.

  • If you elect me - I will fight to keep ITU great. I will love to hear your inputs for the long term goals.

  • The ITU feeling is worth fighting for!

Who am I

My name is Christian and I'm a student at ITU. I love to be busy and therefore I also actively seek new challenges. I have been a tutor for the new BSc students three years in a row. Moreover, I am also part of ScrollBar, where I currently sit on the board and control the finances. However, I am stepping down to focus on joining the board of directors. It is great to see how we as students try to thrive and how engaged we are to ensure we all feel welcome both through organizations but especially with the volunteering tutors/buddies. I love adventure and I try to seek new adventures as often as possible. This is also why I scuba dive and have taken several advanced courses through the years. If you know of a great adventure, hit me up!

What do I study

I study Computer Science in my first semester and I also did my bachelor's in software development at ITU. I am currently interested in picking the software engineering specialization since I enjoy being involved in decision-making and management.

Where do I work

I currently work at a fintech startup called Debbie. We automate and create software for a better debt collection process. We try to ensure customers/debtors get a good experience instead of a lawyer knocking on their door. It is interesting to be part of a startup since you get involved in many aspects of the business. I have also worked at cBrain, where I got just an important view of how bigger companies operate.

Other issues/causes

I would love to help keep ITU great by getting close to the long-term decision-making. Therefore, joining the board of directors is a perfect match to fulfill these goals. The Student Council is already trying to bring focus to mental health and well-being. I would love to bring that further up to the decision-making to ensure such an important issue gets the attention it needs. Moreover, I want to promote climate awareness at ITU to create a more sustainable environment at the university.
